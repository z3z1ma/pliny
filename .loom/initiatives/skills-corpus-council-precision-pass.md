# Objective

Turn the latest council review of `skills/` into a completed, evidence-backed,
critique-reviewed precision pass that removes the remaining ambiguity, drift, and
unsafe defaults from Loom's canonical skill corpus without adding runtime creep,
command-wrapper truth, hidden helper requirements, or new canonical owner layers.

# Why Now

The previous refinement pass closed broad grammar and authority gaps and released
`v0.1.6`. The latest council review found the next quality frontier: small but
high-leverage vocabulary, template, lifecycle, and closure-discipline mismatches
that could still train fresh agents to guess.

# In Scope

- Normalize route vocabulary used by drive/checkpoint/ticket/workspace surfaces.
- Decide and document `.loom/support/` as an optional support surface or remove
  saved support-surface ambiguity.
- Add ticket-level retrospective/promotion disposition to the closure dossier.
- Add initiative cues for delegated authority and objective-level stop conditions.
- Align shared packet grammar and packet templates.
- Strengthen critique/wiki packet lifecycle reconciliation.
- Canonicalize or explicitly document `review_target` shapes.
- Harden templates against saved placeholder pollution.
- Strengthen evidence-quality guidance and ticket evidence teaching.
- Remove the drive handoff `write_scope` collision.
- Improve plan acceptance coverage and parallel-wave readiness checks.
- Replace optional-command wording with route/owner-layer wording where command
  surfaces could be mistaken for protocol truth.

# Out Of Scope

- Do not add a hidden runtime, daemon, database, MCP dependency, required CLI,
  schema engine, or command wrapper as protocol truth.
- Do not create a new canonical owner layer.
- Do not rewrite the entire corpus for style alone.
- Do not treat `examples/`, `.opencode/`, optional utilities, generated files, or
  external trackers as the product surface for this pass.
- Do not weaken ticket-ledger, packet-support, evidence, critique, wiki, or memory
  ownership boundaries.

# Success Metrics

- OBJ-001: Drive/checkpoint/ticket/workspace route vocabulary has one canonical,
  grep-friendly grammar and downstream surfaces stop using conflicting tokens.
- OBJ-002: `.loom/support/` handling is explicit, optional, lazy-materialized,
  non-canonical, and discoverable anywhere workspace shape or drive handoffs are
  taught.
- OBJ-003: Tickets provide a standard retrospective/promotion disposition home for
  closure decisions beyond wiki-only disposition.
- OBJ-004: Initiative records cue delegated authority, autonomy limits, and
  objective-level stop conditions when drive-style autonomous work is delegated.
- OBJ-005: Packet frontmatter grammar and Ralph/critique/wiki packet templates are
  aligned on required/default fields, ID/filename guidance, change/risk class, and
  freshness expectations.
- OBJ-006: Critique and wiki packet workflows require explicit parent merge
  targets or rationale and terminal packet lifecycle reconciliation.
- OBJ-007: `review_target` usage is canonicalized or explicitly documented across
  critique records and critique packets.
- OBJ-008: Templates make placeholder values unmistakably unsafe to save and avoid
  generic claim prose that looks acceptance-ready.
- OBJ-009: Evidence guidance defines freshness, scope, environment, support vs
  challenge strength, limitations, invalidation/supersession, and ticket evidence
  examples strongly enough for acceptance decisions.
- OBJ-010: Drive handoff proposal-time write scope no longer collides with packet
  write-scope grammar.
- OBJ-011: Plan templates and references cue acceptance coverage and parallel-wave
  independence/write-scope checks.
- OBJ-012: Command/adaptor wording does not present optional commands as peers to
  owner layers or workflow routes.
- OBJ-013: Every child ticket is closed with evidence, oracle critique,
  retrospective disposition, semantic commit, and push.

# Milestones

- Milestone 1: Owner records and 12 bounded tickets exist.

- Milestone 3: Packet and critique grammar surfaces are aligned.
- Milestone 4: Template, evidence, drive handoff, and plan guidance are hardened.

- Route vocabulary normalization can accidentally over-constrain useful prose if it
  tries to make every phrase an enum.
- Support-surface documentation can make support artifacts appear canonical unless
  non-ownership language is repeated clearly.
- Template hardening can become noisy if placeholders overwhelm usable examples.
- Drive and packet cleanup can blur sibling workflow boundaries if Ralph language
  leaks into critique/wiki packet workflows.
