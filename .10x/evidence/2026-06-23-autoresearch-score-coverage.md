The observations support that every initial scenario has at least one saved
fixture, S001-S006 have supported first-pass offline scoring, S007 and S008 are
partial, S009 is unsupported with a reason, generated artifacts carry confidence
and limits, and the README records manual inspection notes for positive and
negative matches across S001-S006.

## Limits

This evidence does not show that:

- Offline scores are calibrated beyond Trust Level 1.
- S007 and S008 can support verdicts without human review and repeated-run
  evidence.
- S009 can be scored without cost telemetry and calibrated quality scores.
- The scorer catches all false positives from copied policy language.
- MICRO runner, FULL harness, report generation, subject-agent execution, live
  APIs, or Trust Level 2/3 calibration exist.

Scenario support status is documented in `autoresearch/README.md` and
`autoresearch/offline_score.py`. Score support status is also present in
`autoresearch/catalogs/scores.json`.

Generated `__pycache__` files were created by Python during tests and are ignored
by git; they were excluded from source scans.
